18.1   The Viability Score Formula: V = α + λ∆ − µ(1 − α)σ

Definition 18.1 (Viability Score). The Viability Score V is the operational summary
statistic of the commercial identity model:

                             V = α + λ∆ − µ(1 − α)σ                          (18.1)

where µ = 0.25 is the standard ambiguity burden constant. V combines the pos-
itive contributions of alignment and converted differentiation with the negative
contribution of the ambiguity penalty.

                     18.2. THE FOUR VIABILITY BANDS AND WHAT THEY PREDICT


The Viability Score is a compressed form of the master equation — it does not capture
temporal dynamics, execution effort, or Resolution Pressure. It answers a single
practical question: Given this name and this proposition, is this entity structurally viable?
It provides a fast, consistent, comparable score for any named entity that can be
applied before launch, during a naming audit, or as part of a competitor analysis.




18.2     The Four Viability Bands and What They Predict


18.2.1   Stable Atom: V ≥ 0.85



   STABLE ATOM                                                                 V ≥ 0.85
  What it means: The entity’s naming and differentiation combination achieves
  or approaches full resolution across all four fields. The algorithm maintains
  the entity. Marketing investment compounds rather than compensates.
  Commercial predictions:
    • Organic search visibility achievable within weeks to months of launch

    • Registry classification automatic and accurate

    • New customer acquisition cost at category baseline or below

    • Marketing investment builds compounding returns

    • Entity survives 90-day marketing pauses without significant visibility loss
   Strategic implication: Maintain. Invest in execution and differentiation to
   raise the ceiling. Do not rename.

18.2.2   Transitional Entity: 0.65 ≤ V < 0.85




  TRANSITIONAL ENTITY                                             0.65 ≤ V < 0.85
  What it means: The entity is partially resolved. One or two components
  are missing or weak. The entity is visible but not autonomous — it requires
  ongoing effort to maintain visibility, and that effort produces returns, but at a
  cost multiplier above baseline.
  Commercial predictions:
   • Organic visibility achievable but requires 3–12 months of consistent effort

   • Registry classification partially accurate; may require manual confirmation

   • Customer acquisition cost 1.5–2.5× category baseline

   • Marketing investment produces returns but does not fully compound

   • 90-day marketing pause causes moderate visibility decline that recovers
     slowly
  Strategic implication: Evaluate the cost-benefit of adding the missing resolu-
  tion component. In most cases, a minor name refinement moves the entity to
  Stable Atom and eliminates the ongoing cost multiplier permanently.

18.2.3   High Effort Entity: 0.45 ≤ V < 0.65




  HIGH EFFORT ENTITY                                              0.45 ≤ V < 0.65
  What it means: The entity faces significant structural resistance. Two or more

  Commercial predictions:

   • 90-day marketing pause causes significant visibility collapse
  Strategic implication: Calculate the annual Inference Tax. In virtually all cases,

  The rename conversation should happen now, not after the tax has been paid
  for another year.

18.6   Scoring ∆: The Differentiation Force Rubric

Score each component of ∆ = U · M · T on the following criteria:
Uniqueness (U = 1 − Oc ):

 • U ≥ 0.80: The proposition is genuinely non-substitutable. No competitor makes
   the same specific claim with the same specific mechanism.

 • 0.50 ≤ U < 0.80: The proposition is distinctive but has some competitive
   overlap.

 • U < 0.50: The proposition is either common or easily duplicated. “Great service,”
   “personalized attention,” and “trusted partner” all score U < 0.20.

Memorability (M ):

 • M ≥ 0.80: Someone who heard the proposition once could accurately restate it
   30 days later.

 • 0.50 ≤ M < 0.80: The core idea is retained but details may fade.

 • M < 0.50: The proposition requires re-exposure to recall accurately.

 The Score Is a Prediction, Not an Opinion

 A Viability Score is not a subjective assessment of whether a name is “good.”
 It is a model-derived prediction of the structural conditions the entity will
 face in the commercial resolution environment. Two people applying the
 rubric consistently to the same name will produce the same score. The score is
 falsifiable: it predicts specific commercial outcomes that can be verified against
 actual business performance data.
